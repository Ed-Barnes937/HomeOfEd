2a Multi-tine rake — 4 parallel grooves + carved ridge/furrow shading (the classic rake head)
4 tines · wide comb
Rake head = 4 pins. Each traces the hypotrochoid, offset along its normal, so the pattern is a ribbon of parallel troughs — not a wire.
2b Deep relief — 3 bold tines, strong raking light, sand piled into high ridges
3 tines · deep furrow
Fewer, deeper grooves. Low-angle light casts a hard shadow on one wall and a bright lip on the other — sculptural, most physical.
2c Fine comb — 7 close tines, thin lines, moiré where passes overlap (most meditative)
7 tines · fine comb
Many fine tines packed close. Where the curve crosses itself the combs interfere into shimmering moiré fields — very calm, very "sand".
